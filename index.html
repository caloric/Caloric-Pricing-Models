Introduction to Caloric Pricing Models

Caloric Pricing is a method where the cost of the food is based on the caloric content of the food, not the type or consumer demand.

Caloric Pricing

A Standard Price of 8¢'s per Calorie as of

The Cheeseburger pictured has an estimated 1,300 calories

The market price for this Cheesburger (following the Cheeseburger Index) is: $10.99

NOTE: The "Cheeseburger Index" is a reference to the "Big Mac Index" that Economists use. For clarification, more information can be found on the Big Mac or Cheeseburger Index by clicking here

The cheeseburger index was originally used as a quick global currency exchange standard, but currently it is most commonly used to predict changes in future market movements.

Using the same methodology we can calculate the average price per calorie from the cost of a cheeseburger in every country in the world. Then we can apply that price to all of the other food. There are several exceptions to this rule and several vegetables will have a bottom base price so agricultural communities don't suffer under this plan.

Todays calculated price per Calorie in the US is: $0.08 or $1 per 118 calories

Why is this important?:

Studies show that the cost to eat healthy foods and a healthy amount of calories cost the average US family 240% more than a "junk-food" diet.

These links are provided for your reference: Live Science and London's Telegraph | Please note that these references are a few of many, that support the fact that an unhealthy diet is much more expensive than a healthy one.

With this change to food costs, families grocery store or marketplace purchases would be inline with the amount of calories the family needs (or wants) rather than underpaying for empty calories or overpaying for a healthy sensible diet. This will allow individuals and families the opportunity to create a dietary budget based on their actual caloric needs.

The Results:

by switching to Caloric Based Pricing we saved $0.00 on our weekly grocery bill and were empowered with a more clear and concise way of orgainizing our diet and our budget.

Why it won't work

  • We do things the way they have been done, for this reason change is difficult.
  • The US is a capitalist country, if there is a profit to be made for selling a food item for a price that outweighs its caloric content, then that is what will happen under capitalist guidlines.
  • The inverse is also true, with a functioning economic environment based upon consumer demand a vendor is unlikely to raise the price of their high calorie food item to "get inline" with caloric pricing.
  • Caloric Pricing can easily be attacked for not providing enough of a economic cushion to agricultural vendors. For this reason the "cushion" would have to be sufficient and that can raise it's own set of complications.

Why it will work

  • Caloric Pricing needs to work because at it's root it makes sense. It will inherently make it easier for people to budget their food needs based on their required caloric intake.
  • Caloric Pricing will work because it demystifies caloric energy and the rocky financial impact it can have on the average diet.
  • Caloric Pricing will give the fast food industry the fuel it needs to get to their $15 an hour campaign (for more info on this please click here).
  • Although guaranteed to be difficult at 1st Caloric Pricing provides the needed economic stabilization to further economic growth by selling food items for a price that is equillavent to the value it will provide.
