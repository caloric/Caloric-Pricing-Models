Introduction to Caloric Pricing Models

Caloric Pricing is a method where the cost of the food is based on the caloric content of the food, not the type or consumer demand.

Example of Caloric Pricing

The Cheeseburger pictured has an estimated $xxx.xx calories

The market price for this Cheesburger (following the Cheeseburger Index) is: $xxx.xx

NOTE: The "Cheeseburger Index" is a reference to the "Big Mac Index" that Economists use. For clarification, more information can be found on the Big Mac or Cheeseburger Index by clicking here

The cheeseburger index was originally used as a quick global currency exchange standard, but currently it is most commonly used to predict changes in future market movements.

Using the same methodology we can calculate the average price per calorie from the cost of a cheeseburger in every country in the world. Then we can apply that price to all of the other food. There are several exceptions to this rule and several vegetables will have a bottom base price so agricultural communities don't suffer under this plan.

Todays calculated price per Calorie in the US is: $0.02

Why:

Studies show that the cost to eat healthy foods and a healthy amount of calories cost the average US family 240% more than a "junk-food" diet.

With this change to food costs families food cost would be inline with the amount of calories the family needs (or wants) rather than underpaying for empty calories or overpaying for a healthy sensible diet.
